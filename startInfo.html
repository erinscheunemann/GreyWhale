Summer in the Artic

Just what is a self-respecting whale doing in the cold artic waters?


Polar regions are rich in whale food. The long days and abundant sunlight of the summer favor phytoplankton growth. During this time, cold water from the bottom moves up toward the surface. The cold water is full of the nutrients plankton need to grow. Lots of nutrients, lots of light cause a rapid increase in plankton numbers; the plankton "blooms".


There are lots of these tiny plants available to be eaten. As a result, zooplankton populations also explode. Other animal populations, including the bottom dwellers, also increase. These populations of bottom dwellers provide food for California gray whales.


So, grey whales spend their summers in cold arctic waters because so much food is available.


Shorter days and falling temperatures signal that it is time for the whales to stop feeding. They turn south and begin their great swim.
